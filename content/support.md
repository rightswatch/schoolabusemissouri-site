## Crisis Resources

**RAINN National Sexual Assault Hotline**: 1-800-656-HOPE (4673) — 24/7 confidential support, online chat at rainn.org

**Crisis Text Line**: Text HOME to 741741 — available 24/7

**Missouri Coalition Against Domestic and Sexual Violence**: 573-634-4161 — connects survivors with local providers

## For Children and Families

**Missouri Children's Division**: 573-751-4247 — child protective services, safety planning, and family support

**Childhelp National Child Abuse Hotline**: 1-800-422-4453 — 24/7 crisis intervention and referrals

**National Center for Missing & Exploited Children**: 1-800-843-5678 — resources for families dealing with child exploitation

## Trauma-Informed Therapy

Child sexual abuse trauma requires specialized therapy. Look for therapists trained in:

- **Trauma-Focused Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (TF-CBT)** — evidence-based, validated for child trauma
- **EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing)** — effective for trauma processing
- **Child-Adult Relationship Enhancement (CARE)** — for families

The Missouri Division of Behavioral Health maintains a provider directory at dmh.mo.gov.

## School Accommodations

Survivors experiencing academic difficulties due to trauma may be entitled to:

- **Section 504 plans** — accommodations for students with disabilities (trauma/PTSD qualifies)
- **IEP (Individualized Education Program)** — if the trauma affects educational progress
- **Temporary homebound instruction** — if returning to the school environment is unsafe or traumatizing

Request these accommodations in writing from the school's special education department.

## Supporting a Survivor

If your child has disclosed abuse:
- Believe them — false disclosures of child sexual abuse are extremely rare
- Remain calm — your reaction significantly affects their willingness to continue talking
- Do not question or probe — let trained professionals conduct forensic interviews
- Reassure them it is not their fault
- Connect them with professional support promptly
