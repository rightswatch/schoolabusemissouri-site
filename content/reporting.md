## Step 1: Immediate Safety

If a child is in immediate danger, call **911**.

For non-emergency situations, remove the child from contact with the alleged abuser while you proceed with reporting.

## Step 2: Missouri Child Abuse Hotline

Call the **Missouri Division of Social Services Child Abuse and Neglect Hotline**: **1-800-392-3738** (available 24 hours/day, 7 days/week).

A trained specialist will take your report. You do not need proof — you only need reasonable cause to suspect abuse. Reports made in good faith are protected from civil liability.

## Step 3: Local Law Enforcement

Report to your local police department or sheriff's office. Child sexual abuse by a school employee is a felony. Law enforcement can:
- Conduct a criminal investigation
- Coordinate with the prosecutor's office
- Interview the child in a child-advocacy center setting (reduces trauma)

## Step 4: School Administration / Title IX Coordinator

File a written complaint with the school's **Title IX Coordinator**. This triggers the school's obligation to investigate independently of any criminal process.

Keep copies of everything. Note dates and times of all communications.

## Step 5: Missouri DESE

File a complaint with the **Missouri Department of Elementary and Secondary Education** at dese.mo.gov. DESE can investigate educator conduct and take action against teaching certificates.

## Step 6: U.S. Department of Education OCR

For federally funded schools, file a complaint with the **Office for Civil Rights** at ocrcas.ed.gov. **180-day deadline** from the incident or last act of discrimination.

## Documentation Checklist

- Written account of what happened, when, and who was present
- All communications with the school (emails, letters, meeting notes)
- Any physical evidence
- Names of potential witnesses
- Medical or counseling records (if child has been seen)
- Screenshots of any electronic communications

## Consult an Attorney

A civil attorney can advise on preserving evidence, meeting deadlines, and protecting your family's legal rights throughout the reporting process.
