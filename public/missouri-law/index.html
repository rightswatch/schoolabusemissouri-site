Missouri School Abuse Laws

Overview of Missouri statutes governing school sexual abuse, mandatory reporting, institutional liability, and the statute of limitations for survivors.

By Rights Watch Media Group • January 1, 0001 • 2 min read

No Statute of Limitations for Childhood Sexual Abuse

Missouri Revised Statutes § 537.046 provides that civil claims for childhood sexual abuse may be brought regardless of when the abuse occurred. Missouri eliminated the statute of limitations for such claims, meaning adult survivors can bring civil actions against both perpetrators and institutions even decades after the abuse.

This applies to:

  • Claims against the individual abuser
  • Claims against schools, districts, churches, and other institutions that employed, supervised, or enabled the abuser

Mandatory Reporting — § 210.115

Missouri law designates certain professionals as mandatory reporters required to report suspected child abuse or neglect. All school personnel — teachers, administrators, coaches, counselors, support staff — are mandatory reporters.

Key requirements:

  • Report must be made to the Missouri Division of Social Services (1-800-392-3738) within 24 hours
  • Report may be made orally initially, but must be followed up in writing
  • Failure to report is a Class A misdemeanor (first offense) or Class E felony (subsequent offenses)
  • Reporters are immune from civil liability if the report is made in good faith

School District Liability

Missouri courts recognize claims against school districts for:

  • Negligent hiring — failing to screen employees for prior abuse history
  • Negligent supervision — failing to supervise employees with known risk factors
  • Negligent retention — continuing to employ an employee after warning signs
  • Title IX deliberate indifference — for federally funded schools that knew and failed to respond

Criminal Statutes

Missouri § 566.083 prohibits sexual contact between school employees and students. This is a felony regardless of consent — students cannot consent to sexual contact with school employees.

Missouri § 566.086 covers sexual misconduct involving a child, and § 566.067 covers child molestation.
